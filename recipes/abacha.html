Abacha

Description

Abacha is a native African spicy dish which is originated from the Igbo tribe of the eastern part of Nigeria. Abacha is simply dried shreded cassava.

Abacha is a delight to eat and easy to make, that is why it is used as an appetitizer and significant delicacy in most igbo ocassions.

Ingredient

  • Abacha
  • Ugba
  • Palm oil
  • Potash
  • Fresh fish
  • Stock fish
  • Scotch bonnet
  • Garden egg
  • Utazi leaves
  • Cray fish powder
  • Calabash nutmeg seed
  • Ogiri
  • Knorr Seasoning cube

Steps

  2. Then grill for 45 minutes on high.
  3. Soak the Abacha in cold water till soft, this should take roughly 10 minutes, and leave to drain in a colander.
  4. Rinse the Ugba with warm water to get rid of any dirt.
  5. Boil the stockfish with salt and seasoning till soft.
  6. Dissolve the potash in 1 cup of water.
  7. Blend the scotch bonnet, 1/2 of the onion, crayfish, and ground Ehuru till smooth and set aside.
  8. Roast the Calabash nutmeg, then peel and grind it in a mortar or mill.
  9. Pour the palm oil into a bowl or saucepan, gradually add the potash mixture. Mix thoroughly. You\’ll notice the palm oil will begin to thicken and the color will turn yellowy. Be careful not to add too much and be careful not to add the residue at the bottom of the bowl.
  10. Now, add the pepper mix into the palm oil.
  11. Now, add the shredded stockfish, Ogiri, then the Ugba, stir and combine.
  12. Then add the Abacha, stir and combine. And it\’s ready.
